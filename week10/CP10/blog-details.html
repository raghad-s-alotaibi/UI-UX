Why Glycerin Is A Must-Have For Deeply Hydrating Your Skin

  • By Deercreative
  • February 21, 2019
  • 8 Comments
share

You might not know it yet, but glycerin is your dry skin’s best friend. In fact, it’s the third most common ingredient in skin care formulas, and for good reason. Glycerin works to hydrate the outer layer of the skin, improving skin barrier function and accelerating the healing process, all without irritation.

So, what is glycerin, exactly? It’s a clear, colorless humectant naturally derived from vegetable oils (and sometimes animal fats). Humectants, like hyaluronic acid, allantoin and glycerin, help to draw water into the skin and keep it there — a must-have first step for treating dehydrated skin.

Glycerin is extremely common across the skin care world, but a lot of products designed for sensitive and dry skin feature glycerin right at the top of their ingredient lists thanks to its ability to act as a moisture magnet. Here are a few of our favorite K-Beauty stand-outs that will give your skin a healthy dose of glycerin goodness.

Glycerin is a favorite for sheet masks as it works to hydrate while its viscous texture helps keep masks locked in place. This mask from Klairs is beloved for its barrier-repairing abilities thanks to the addition of ceramides, cooling aloe vera and soothing centella asiatica extract. Keep it in the fridge and pop it on when your skin is in need of some TLC.

Glycerin gives hydrating and exfoliating toners a comfortable “slip” on the skin, helping to prep it for more intensive treatments.
